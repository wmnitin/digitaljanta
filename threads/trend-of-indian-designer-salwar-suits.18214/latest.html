The Salwar Suit is one of the most popular clothes for millions of women in Asian territory. Designer Salwar Suits look elegant for a wedding ceremony or even for casual family gatherings. This outfit has adapted for all occasions as casual or formal because of its availability in diverse fabric, color, and embroidery options. The designer Salwar Kameez is fast becoming the main attraction across the globe. The ethnic styles of designer suits have taken the fashion trends to the next level. We can see a large variety of suits and salwars available in the market because of culture diversity.



The Suit is a long top or tunic, which often reaches up to the knees, but length can vary according to the latest fashion trends. There are different kinds of Salwar suits available in the market like Anarkali, Patiala, palazzo, Churidar, gowns, jacket Kurta and many others that differ in the cut of the Kameez or the making of the Salwar. We have options to choose the style of the neck, the length of the Kameez, the style of the sleeves, the print and many other things which lead to an impressive look to outfit according to the occasion. Each different style in Salwar kameez involves a different grace, charm and exquisite. Suits are flouting to the market with the peculiarity in embroideries, pearls, gems, laces and motifs found in different regions, being stitched in fabrics.

Let's discuss each type of Salwar suit one by one in detail:

Anarkali Suits

Presently, Anarkali Suits are becoming very prominent all over India and other parts of the world. Fashion trends have given a special place for traditional outfits wore by women. Returning to the golden era with the ethnic Salwar Kameez fashion brings out the Mughal era. A popular Mughal courtesan by the name of Anarkali augmented the grand enigma and proposal of the outfit. Starting from the past historical times to the present generation, Anarkali Dress has undergone numerous changes in terms of designs, colors, patterns and creative ideologies.



The Anarkali suit is designed with a frock-style top, without any slit at the side, long length and team up with a stylish slim fitted bottom. This type of suit varies in its length and embroidery work. Women prefer heavily embroidered suits adorn on various occasions or wedding functions. Anarkali suit refers to the flowing salwar suits which are of full length and are designed like frocks without any type of slit at the side.

These are made up of silk, cotton, net, satin, georgette,chiffon, brasso and other materials. Nowadays, the ones that are popular include net materials. The sleeves can also be made of the net material or the material that contribute the inner linings. The sleeve length also varies. Full sleeves look good with this type of kameez. The Neck has heavy embroidery of Sequins, beads, laces, etc with full sleeves.

Patiala Suits

Among the different range of salwar suits, patiala salwar suit is one of the most trending styles that has emerged in the fashion world. Although, patiala salwar kameez is known as the original dress from Punjab but with increasing popularity, this style of dressing has become one of the most favourite in Asian territory. Patiala suit is a three piece attire including kameez, salwar and a dupatta.



The upper part in the set is known as kameez, while the bottom piece of clothing is known as the salwar. Patiala salwar is like the normal salwar kameez but with a slight difference in the appearence. Patiala salwar has short and fitted kameez while the salwar has numerous drapes that fall fluidly to ankle length team up with a graceful phulkari dupatta and long dangling jhumki earrings. The modern woman has included this style in her wardrobe and wearing it as casual and formal occasion wear.

Palazzo Suits

The key to looking trendy in salwar suit designs is to feel comfortable wearing them. Palazzos are all about comfort with style with cool and chic summer look. Palazzo Suit is long straight pant which is tight at the beginning and loose at the bottom and wide. Palazzo Suits are little bit different from all other pants like bell bottoms, Gaucho Trousers, and Parallels.



It is the newest Indo-western trend adopted from the flared silhouettes of the sixties & seventies. They come in an elegant fusion of modern prints with ethnic motifs, with and without embroidery. The palazzo pant sounds good with heavy Indian embroidered tunics, tops and long kurtas.
